# Tennoplan Design System — Impeccable v3

*Authoritative source. Read before building any UI. Replaces v2 (now at `.impeccable.legacy.md` for reference).*

*Last updated: 2026-05-31 — composition-over-containment rewrite, Geist family adopted, density modes introduced, UI Pro Max engineering rules folded inline.*

---

## 1. Project & Users

**Tennoplan** is an offline-first Warframe companion. Tauri 2 + React 19 + Vite + Cloudflare. The audience is **endgame Warframe players** — grinders, theory-crafters, build-planners, content-trackers. Serious, focused, second-monitor users who want **information per pixel** maximized without it feeling cramped.

Two usage modes the design must serve simultaneously:

1. **Glance (≤5s)** — scan a surface and absorb the key state without focusing. Timer is X. Mod count is Y. Frame is Ready.
2. **Deep dive (2+ min)** — explore breakdowns, stat formulas, build math, drop tables, lore.

If a layout helps one mode at the expense of the other, it's wrong.

## 2. Brand & Aesthetic Direction

### What it feels like

> *"This looks amazing. The text is easy to read. So presentable. Like infographics, but better. The person who made this really understood what we need as players."*

**Polished. Informational. Distinctive. Power-user dense.**

### Theme — Mutalist Glow (locked)

Deep green-black base, bioluminescent jade accent, warm amber gold accent. We don't read as "another Warframe app with Lotus cyan and Orokin gold" — we read as the **Heart of Deimos / Cambion Drift / Helminth Chair** aesthetic. Lore-coded, immediately recognizable as Tennoplan.

The 7 brand color tokens (§4) are theme-swappable. Future themes (Steel Path Crimson, Void Operator, Orokin Tower) re-map the same 7 tokens — no structural changes. Themes are a planned major feature; never hardcode colors outside these tokens.

### Anti-references (what we are explicitly NOT)

- ❌ Alecaframe / generic neon-aggressive Warframe apps
- ❌ Generic SaaS dashboards with "card box garbage"
- ❌ Floating rounded-rectangle drop-shadow templates
- ❌ Glassmorphism / gradient text / side-stripe borders (see §10)
- ❌ Marketing-app type sizes (51px h1, 16px body) — that's a landing-page scale, not a power-user tool

## 3. The Core Principle — Composition over Containment

**This is the spine of the system. Every other rule serves it.**

The pain in v2 was that everything ended up in boxes — a portrait box next to a stats box next to an abilities box. Each card its own island. The result feels generic regardless of how well-typeset the contents are. The reader's eye sees rectangles, not information.

The new rule:

> **Artwork doesn't take space. It adds to the information.**
> **Art and info are composed together, not placed side-by-side in separate boxes.**
> **If everything is full of resources, where does the information live?**

Artwork is the **gestalt anchor** — the visual gravitational center. Information **emerges from** that anchor and has full layout freedom. The artwork is positioned, often **offset to one side or bleeding from an edge**, so info radiates from it without being constrained by its silhouette.

**The artwork is artwork-anchored, NOT artwork-constrained.** Text doesn't weave around a centered character's limbs (that would limit information). The character is **scooted to a side, bleeds from a corner, or anchors from a rail**, and the data has room to flow.

### Three sanctioned composition patterns

#### 3a. Anchored Composition (hero & entry surfaces)

Used for: Warframe entry, Weapon entry, Arcane detail, Companion detail, hero moments.

- The artwork is a **structural anchor**, not boxed content. It bleeds, overlaps, or extends to one edge.
- Position it **off-center** — left rail, right rail, or breaking the top-right corner. Almost never dead-center.
- Stats, abilities, descriptions occupy whatever space they need. They don't wrap around limbs.
- The artwork's lit areas can bleed into a background gradient that informs the composition's color story.
- Boundaries between "image" and "data" dissolve — a stat label can sit on the background gradient, an ability glyph can overlap the artwork's edge, a section header can be set against the artwork's negative space.
- The artwork's silhouette **does not define a forbidden zone for text**.

#### 3b. Editorial Rail (Fashion Frame poster style)

Used for: stat sidebars, identity blocks, accompanying small-caps detail next to a hero composition.

- Inspired by Warframe Fashion Frame posters: `PRIMARY / SECONDARY / TERTIARY / ACCENTS / EMISSIVE I / EMISSIVE II` stacked vertically with generous letter-spacing.
- Small caps, light weight, muted color. The labels recede; the values pop.
- No box around the rail — separated from the composition only by typographic rhythm and spacing.
- Pairs with anchored compositions on entry pages.

#### 3c. Inventory Grid (DIM-dense)

Used for: codex landing tiles, mods grid, weapon list, resource list, browse surfaces.

- Tiny icon tiles, color-coded backgrounds, **6–8 columns at 1080p**.
- Hover for detail, click to enter. No verbose labels on the tile itself.
- Tile size is proportional to content weight, not uniform (see §6, Tile-Sizing-by-Content).
- Pinned items and Recently Added rails surface as the same tile shape, just in a horizontal scroller above the main grid.

### What this is NOT

- ❌ `[artwork card] [stats card]` side-by-side. That's containment, not composition.
- ❌ Centered hero portrait with stats wrapped tightly around its silhouette. That constrains info.
- ❌ Uniform card grid where every card is `[icon] [name] [meta]` regardless of what the entry actually needs.
- ❌ "Background image" treatment where artwork is just decoration behind a normal layout.
- ❌ Box-in-box. Never nest a card inside a card. Never wrap an artwork in a card. Never wrap a stat in a card inside a section that's already in a card.

### How to know you got it right

Ask: *"Could I lift the artwork off this page and the layout would still feel intentional?"* If yes, the artwork was an island and you failed. *"Would removing the stats change the visual composition?"* If no, the info wasn't really woven in.

## 4. Color System

### Brand palette (Mutalist Glow — default, theme-swappable)

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| `--color-bg-primary` | `#080a08` | Deep green-black — main background, surface-lowest |
| `--color-bg-secondary` | `#13180f` | Dark green surface — elevated panels, content containers |
| `--color-accent-gold` | `#dbb058` | Warm amber — emphasis, key info, primary accent (~10–15% visual weight) |
| `--color-accent-jade` | `#58e88a` | Bioluminescent — interactive states, live data, secondary accent |
| `--color-text-primary` | `#e3e8de` | Cool cream — body text, labels |
| `--color-text-muted` | `#959e8a` | Sage-gray — secondary info, supporting text |
| `--color-border-default` | `#252e23` | Green-shadow — structural lines, dividers |

These **7 tokens** are the entire theme surface. Future themes (Steel Path Crimson, Void Operator, etc.) re-map exactly these 7 — no other color values are theme-dependent.

Legacy alias `--color-accent-teal` maps to `--color-accent-jade` and stays available until call sites migrate.

### Stat tints (semantic, theme-independent)

These map to in-game UI conventions and never shift with themes.

| Token | Hex | Stat |
|---|---|---|
| `--color-stat-health` | `#e25c5c` | Warm red — HP |
| `--color-stat-shield` | `#b07cd6` | Lavender — Shield (deliberately not in-game cyan so it doesn't clash with brand) |
| `--color-stat-energy` | `#6da8e8` | Soft blue — Energy |

### Color usage rules

- Use OKLCH or `color-mix()` for derived shades, not HSL. OKLCH is perceptually uniform.
- Tint neutrals toward the brand hue. A subtle 0.005–0.01 chroma shift creates subconscious cohesion.
- **60-30-10 by visual weight, not pixel count.** ~60% surface, ~30% secondary text and borders, ~10% accent. Gold and jade work *because* they're rare.
- Gold is accent, **not background**. Never use gold as a fill on large surfaces.
- Jade signals interactivity / live / interactive feedback. Reserve it.
- Never pure black (`#000`) or pure white (`#fff`). Always tinted.
- Stat tints stay independent — Energy is blue, Health is red, Shield is lavender, regardless of theme.

## 5. Typography — Geist family + Noto Serif

### Font stack

| Family | Role | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Geist Sans** | Body, UI, labels, default typography | `geist` npm package |
| **Geist Mono** | Numeric columns, stat values, timers, monospace data | `geist` npm package |
| **Geist Pixel** (Square / Grid / Circle / Triangle / Line) | Ornament glyph system — dividers, polarity markers, status pips, micro-decoration | `geist` npm package |
| **Noto Serif** | Display headlines, editorial rail labels, hero composition typography | Google Fonts |

**Inter is banned.** It was the v2 body font; it's also on impeccable's reflex-fonts-to-reject list. Geist Sans replaces it because it's distinctive without being a reflex pick, and **the Pixel variants make the family non-generic** — that's the signature.

### Implementation (Vite, not Next.js)

The `geist` package ships `.woff2` files in `node_modules/geist/dist/fonts/`. Wire via `@font-face` in `src/index.css` pointing to those paths (or use a Vite font plugin). The Next.js `import { GeistSans } from 'geist/font/sans'` pattern won't work — that's framework-specific. Same fonts, different glue.

### Geist Pixel — the ornament system

This is the differentiating piece. Five pixel-shape variants (Square, Grid, Circle, Triangle, Line) used as **iconographic accents**, not body text. Concrete deployments:

- **Polarity markers** in mod tiles and slot indicators (each polarity gets a Pixel glyph signature).
- **Section bullets** before editorial rail labels (e.g. `▢ PRIMARY  ▢ SECONDARY  ▢ TERTIARY`).
- **Status pips** (Ready / Coming Soon / Live).
- **Dividers** — pixel-line glyphs as section separators instead of solid `<hr>`.
- **Search input prefix glyph**.
- **Pinned-item flag**.
- **Hero composition micro-ornaments** punctuating editorial rail labels.
- **Loading states** — animated cycling Pixel shapes.

Geist Pixel is **never used as body text or label text**. It is **always ornament**. This discipline is what keeps it from feeling gimmicky.

### Type scale — 1080p-first, power-user dense

Tennoplan is a desktop tool, not a marketing page. The scale is compressed.

| Token | Size | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Display | `clamp(2rem, 5vw, 3.5rem)` / 32–56px | Hero composition titles only — rare |
| h1 (page) | `1.75rem` / 28px | Tab/page hero ("WARFRAMES", "VOID RELIQUARIES") |
| h2 (section) | `1.25rem` / 20px | Section headers within a page |
| h3 (subsection) | `1rem` / 16px **bold** | Card/composition subsection titles |
| Body | `0.875rem` / 14px | Default body — denser than v2's 16px |
| Label | `0.75rem` / 12px | Field labels, metadata |
| Caption | `0.6875rem` / 11px | Editorial rail small caps, micro-metadata |
| Pixel ornament | `0.625rem` / 10px | Geist Pixel glyph accents |

**Line height inverse to size**:
- Display / h1 / h2 → `1.15` (tight)
- h3 / body → `1.5` (balanced)
- Label / caption → `1.4` (readable despite size)
- **Light text on dark needs +0.05–0.1 to line-height.** Light type reads as lighter weight and wants breathing room.

### Weights

Geist Sans has a variable axis. Use:
- **700 Bold** — page titles, key data, emphasized values
- **500 Medium** — section headers, button labels
- **400 Regular** — body
- **300 Light** — muted secondary, descriptions, captions

Don't use a flat weight range. Span 300–700 for hierarchy.

### Typography rules

- **Tabular numerals everywhere data shows.** Use Geist Mono for numeric columns (mod cost, drop chance, timer, stat values). Or `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums` on Geist Sans for inline numbers. Prevents column shimmer.
- **No all-caps body text.** All-caps reserved for short labels (editorial rail) and short section headers.
- **Noto Serif is reserved** for display/editorial — hero composition titles, Fashion Frame-style rail labels at h3+ size. Not body.
- **Line length 65–75ch max** for body passages. Wider fatigues the eye.
- **No gradient text.** Ever. (See §10.)

## 6. Spacing & Density

### 4pt scale tokens

| Token | Value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `--space-xs` | 4px | Within a row, between icon and adjacent text |
| `--space-sm` | 8px | Between siblings in a tight group |
| `--space-md` | 12px | Default gap inside compositions |
| `--space-lg` | 16px | Between sections within a card |
| `--space-xl` | 24px | Between major sections |
| `--space-2xl` | 32px | Page-level rhythm |
| `--space-3xl` | 48px | Hero composition negative space |
| `--space-4xl` | 64px | Rare — for major editorial pauses |

**Use `gap`, not margins, for sibling spacing.** Eliminates margin collapse.

### Density modes

Two density modes, controlled by a global Zustand setting + media query default.

| Mode | Default trigger | Body | h1 | Gap | Edge padding |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Compact** | < 1440px viewport (1080p users) OR user toggle | 13px | 24px | 8px | 12px |
| **Standard** | ≥ 1440px viewport (default) | 14px | 28px | 12px | 16px |

Spacious mode is not in scope — the system is power-user-first, not accessibility-first. (Accessibility is served by reduced-motion, contrast, focus rings — not by sparse layouts.)

The toggle lives in a Settings panel. CSS reads `data-density="compact"` on `<html>` and applies via CSS variables that override the defaults.

### Tile-Sizing-by-Content contract

**This rule alone fixes the "polarity stretches to 600px" pain.** Every block in a composition declares its sizing intent:

| Intent | CSS | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| `compact` | `width: max-content` or grid column `auto` | Content hugs its data. Polarity badge, count, status pip, single-word stat. |
| `flex` | `flex: 1` or grid column `1fr` | Fills available space. Description text, primary content, name. |
| `hero` | Fixed size, anchors layout, may bleed | Portrait, primary artwork. Defines the composition's center of gravity. |

**Forbidden**: declaring every grid column as `1fr` or every cell with the same padding. That's the v2 mistake.

**Preferred**: explicit mixed column patterns. Example for a Warframe stat row:

```css
.stat-row {
  display: grid;
  grid-template-columns: auto 1fr auto;
  /* label (compact) | meaning (flex) | value (compact) */
  gap: var(--space-sm);
}
```

For inventory grids: explicit column count or `repeat(auto-fill, minmax(120px, 140px))` — **bounded max**, not `1fr`, so tiles don't stretch.

### Subgrid for cross-card alignment

When stat labels need to align across multiple cards (e.g. a row of Warframe summary cards each with `HEALTH / SHIELD / ENERGY`), use CSS subgrid. The labels and values align across the row without visual approximation.

### Container queries for component responsiveness

Components adapt to their **container** width, not the viewport. A Warframe summary card in the right rail (320px) and the same card in the main column (900px) reflow differently. Use `@container` queries, not media queries, on every component.

### Spacing rhythm

- **Same padding everywhere = monotonous.** Vary based on density intent.
- Tight groupings (related items): `--space-xs` to `--space-sm`.
- Major separations (between sections): `--space-xl` to `--space-3xl`.
- Hero compositions can use `--space-4xl` for editorial pause.

## 7. Mixed Geometry Vocabulary

Break the rectangle monotony with intentional shape mixing.

| Shape | Use |
|---|---|
| **Circle** | Portraits, ability frames, avatar surfaces, status pips, ornamental Pixel-Circle glyphs |
| **Rounded square** (radius 4–6px) | Inventory tiles, stat boxes, content tiles |
| **Hexagon / polygon** | Polarity markers (via Geist Pixel), faction sigils, rank tier indicators |
| **Pixel-shape** (Geist Pixel) | Editorial ornaments, dividers, section bullets |

**No floating rounded-rectangle drop-shadow cards.** That's generic AI. Use mixed geometry as a default — round a portrait, square a stat tile, hexagon-mark a polarity, divider-line a section. Within one composition, ≥2 shape families should appear.

## 8. Power-User Patterns

### Density toggle (MVP)

Global setting in `useUiSettingsStore` (Zustand). `density: 'compact' | 'standard'`. Auto-defaults to `compact` under 1440px viewport. User can override in Settings. Persisted to localStorage.

### Pinned items rail (MVP)

User-curated quick-access surface. Pin any codex entry (Warframe, mod, weapon, relic, arcane, resource). Pinned items appear:
- As a horizontal scroller rail above the codex landing grid.
- In a small "Pinned" widget on the Dailies & Weeklies page.
- In a sidebar dropdown for cross-tab access.

Storage: Dexie `pins` table keyed by entry slug.

### Hover-secondary, click-primary

Every tile has:
- **Click** → primary action (enter the page).
- **Hover** → reveal secondary info or actions (pin, compare, jump-to-source). No menu chrome shown by default.

This is how DIM keeps tile chrome minimal while exposing power.

### Keyboard shortcuts (post-MVP)

Planned, not MVP:
- `?` → opens shortcut cheatsheet overlay
- `/` → focuses global search
- `g + w` → jump to Warframes, `g + m` → Mods, `g + r` → Relics, etc.
- `Esc` → close modal / back out of detail

When implemented, the cheatsheet itself becomes a hero composition example — pixel-glyph keycaps, editorial rail of shortcut groups.

### Right-click context menus (post-MVP)

Desktop convention. On any tile: right-click → Pin / Copy slug / Compare / Open Wiki source. Defer until after MVP density + pins ship.

### Recently Added rail (already shipped)

The codex landing's "Recently Added" rail is the prototype for this pattern family. Pinned items use the same tile shape, just user-curated instead of recency-driven.

## 9. Frozen Components Registry

These components are **complete**. Do not alter their internal composition, container, fonts, borders, or layout. Only their **outer context** (the grid that contains them, the spacing around them) is free to change.

| Component | Status | What's frozen | What's free |
|---|---|---|---|
| `ModCardV3` | Frozen | Container, layered piece arrangement, borders, fonts, edge anchoring, all 9 polarity tier visuals | The grid that holds it: column count, gap, edge gutter |
| `ModDetailModal` | Frozen | Internal layout, fonts, borders, content arrangement | Trigger surface, entry/exit animation |

**Mods page itself is free to restructure** — grouping, sorting, filtering, density, header chrome, sidebar context. But never break the card.

Before editing any component in this list: stop. Ask whether the change is to the card itself (forbidden) or the container around it (free). When in doubt, the card stays.

## 10. Anti-Patterns — Forbidden

The v2 spec had some of these. v3 expands the list with the specific dead-space and box-island patterns shown in the user's screenshot.

### Absolute bans

1. ❌ **Side-stripe borders on cards** — `border-left: 3px+` or `border-right: 3px+` with any color. Single most overused dashboard tell.
2. ❌ **Gradient text** — `background-clip: text` + gradient. Decorative noise.
3. ❌ **Glassmorphism** — blur + glass + glow used decoratively rather than purposefully.
4. ❌ **Box-next-to-box** — `[artwork card] + [stats card]` side-by-side. This is the v2 sin §3 forbids.
5. ❌ **Generic uniform card grids** — every card identical `[icon] [name] [meta]` regardless of content type.
6. ❌ **Nested cards** — card inside a card inside a section.
7. ❌ **Hero metric template** — big number / small label / supporting stat / gradient accent. Pure SaaS-template AI slop.
8. ❌ **Dead-space-with-tiny-icon** — a 280px tile holding a 16px icon, a small label, and 200px of empty space (the exact pattern in the screenshot).
9. ❌ **Sparkline as decoration** — tiny charts that look sophisticated but convey nothing.
10. ❌ **Bounce / elastic easing** — feels dated. Use ease-out.
11. ❌ **Neon-on-dark cyber aesthetic** — Tennoplan is infested-organic, not cyberpunk.
12. ❌ **Pure `#000` / `#fff`** — always tinted.
13. ❌ **Inter as a font choice** — banned by both this spec and the impeccable skill.
14. ❌ **Modals for primary navigation** — modals are lazy. Use real surfaces.
15. ❌ **Same padding everywhere** — kills rhythm.
16. ❌ **Centering everything** — left-aligned with asymmetry feels more designed.

### The screenshot-specific bans

These map directly to the issues you pointed out:

- **Padding squishing artwork**: no padding applied to artwork sides of an anchored composition. Artwork bleeds or is offset; the data has its own padding.
- **Tiny icon + huge name + dead space**: tile contents must be **content-sized** (§6 Tile-Sizing-by-Content). Either the icon gets bigger and earns the tile, or the tile shrinks. A 16px icon + word + 200px of dead pixels is forbidden.
- **Polarity row stretching to viewport edge**: explicit `auto` columns or `max-content` width. No `1fr` on compact content.

## 11. Engineering Rules — UI Pro Max Inline

Quantitative rules the implementation must respect. These are baked into the spec; treat them as non-negotiable.

### Accessibility (CRITICAL)

- **Contrast** — body text ≥4.5:1 against background. Large text (≥24px) ≥3:1. Stat tints already verified against `--color-bg-primary`.
- **Focus rings** — every interactive element has a visible focus ring (2px `--color-accent-gold` outline, 2px offset). Never removed.
- **Keyboard nav** — full keyboard support. Tab order matches visual order.
- **Alt text** — every meaningful image. Decorative images get `alt=""`.
- **Color not the only indicator** — pair red/green with shape/icon for colorblind users.
- **`prefers-reduced-motion`** — respect it. Swap entry animations to crossfades.

### Performance

- **Virtualize lists ≥50 items.** Codex has 1801 mods, 3020 relics. Use `react-window` or similar.
- **Reserve image dimensions.** Every `<img>` gets `width × height` attrs or `aspect-ratio`. Prevents CLS.
- **CLS budget < 0.1.** Reserve space for async content.
- **Lazy-load non-hero assets.** Codex artwork below the fold uses `loading="lazy"`.
- **Image format**: WebP for raster, SVG for icons. PNG only when transparency needs it and WebP can't.
- **Font display**: `font-display: swap` on `@font-face` declarations.

### Typography

- **Tabular numerals** on every numeric column. Geist Mono is tabular by default; Geist Sans needs `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums`.
- **Truncation**: prefer wrapping. When truncating, ellipsis + tooltip with full text.

### Motion

- **Duration windows**: micro-interactions 150–300ms, complex transitions ≤400ms, never >500ms.
- **Easing**: `ease-out-quart` / `ease-out-quint` / `ease-out-expo` for entries. `ease-in` for exits.
- **Exit ≤70% of enter** — exit feels responsive.
- **Animate `transform` and `opacity` only.** Never `width / height / margin / top / left` — they cause reflow.
- **For height transitions**: animate `grid-template-rows` (0fr → 1fr), not the element height directly.
- **Stagger** list entrance 30–50ms per item. Not all-at-once.
- **Reduced motion**: respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`. Disable or shorten to instant.

### Layout

- **Container queries** (`@container`) for components. Viewport queries (`@media`) only for top-level page layout.
- **`min-h-dvh` over `100vh`** on mobile fallbacks (Tauri is desktop so rare).
- **Reserve safe padding** for any fixed/sticky element.
- **Spacing scale**: 4pt only. No arbitrary px values.

### Data display

- **Chart type matches data type**: trend → line; comparison → bar; proportion → donut (only if ≤5 categories, else bar); time-series → line with brushable axis.
- **Tooltips on hover (desktop) and tap (mobile).** Show exact values.
- **Legends near the chart, never below the fold.**
- **Empty states** show guidance, not a blank axis frame.
- **Loading states** use skeleton/shimmer, not spinners, for >300ms operations.

### Forms (where they appear — Settings, filters)

- **Visible labels**, not placeholder-only.
- **Inline validation on blur**, not on every keystroke.
- **Error messages near the field** with a recovery path.
- **Helper text persistent**, not just in placeholder.

### Icons & visual elements

- **No emojis as structural icons.** Use Geist Pixel for ornaments and `lucide-react` for UI icons.
- **Consistent icon sizing**: `icon-sm` (16px), `icon-md` (20px), `icon-lg` (24px). No arbitrary 18, 22, 26.
- **Consistent stroke** within an icon set. lucide-react default 1.5px.

## 13. Theming Strategy

Theming is a planned major feature. The 7 brand color tokens (§4) are the entire swap surface. Every other color is derived via `color-mix()` or stat-token (theme-independent).

### How a theme works

```css
[data-theme="mutalist-glow"] {
  --color-bg-primary: #080a08;
  --color-bg-secondary: #13180f;
  --color-accent-gold: #dbb058;
  --color-accent-jade: #58e88a;
  --color-text-primary: #e3e8de;
  --color-text-muted: #959e8a;
  --color-border-default: #252e23;
}

[data-theme="steel-path-crimson"] {
  --color-bg-primary: #0a0708;
  /* ... 6 more values ... */
}
```

Toggle via Zustand store + `<html data-theme="...">`. No structural changes. No component changes. Themes are pure color re-skin.

### Planned themes

- **Mutalist Glow** (default) — deep green-black + jade + amber gold
- **Steel Path Crimson** — near-black + sharper gold + Stalker crimson
- **Void Operator** — deep violet-black + gold + Operator violet
- **Orokin Tower** — warm coffee-onyx + brighter gold + sigil-sage

Don't ship more than one theme initially. Mutalist Glow stays locked as default. Add others when the system is mature.

## 14. Implementation Checklist

When building or migrating a surface, run through:

### Composition
- [ ] Did I default to a box? Could the artwork anchor instead?
- [ ] Is the artwork (if any) off-center / bleeding / anchoring — not centered with text wrapped around?
- [ ] Are there ≥2 shape families in this composition? (circles, squares, polygons, Pixel ornaments)
- [ ] Does every block declare compact / flex / hero intent?
- [ ] Is `1fr` only used on blocks that should fill?

### Typography
- [ ] Geist Sans for body, Geist Mono for numbers, Noto Serif for display, Geist Pixel for ornament — never reversed?
- [ ] Tabular numerals on every numeric column?
- [ ] No Inter, no Fraunces, no DM Sans, no other rejected fonts?
- [ ] Weight range spans at least 300–700?

### Color
- [ ] Only the 7 brand tokens + 3 stat tints + derived `color-mix()` values?
- [ ] No hardcoded hex in component CSS?
- [ ] Contrast verified (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text)?
- [ ] No pure black or pure white?

### Density

## 16. Decisions Log

### 2026-05-31 — v3 rewrite (this document)

**Trigger**: codex landing page showed v2's failure modes — boxes-next-to-boxes, tiny-icon-with-dead-space, uniform tile sizing that stretched compact content to viewport edges. Information was being held hostage by container chrome.

**Core shift**: from "Component Patterns" (containers describing card layouts) to **Composition over Containment** (artwork anchors layouts, info radiates, boxes are the exception). The principle is so central it gets §3 — the spine of the system.

**Typography pivot**: Inter out (banned by user AND by impeccable skill's reflex list). Geist family in — Sans for UI, Mono for numbers, **Pixel as a signature ornament system**. Noto Serif retained for display/editorial. The Pixel variants are the differentiator that stops Geist Sans from being "the new Inter."

**Density introduced**: 1080p-first power-user scale. Compact default under 1440px. Type scale compressed from v2's marketing-app sizes (51px h1) to power-user sizes (28px h1).

**Tile-sizing-by-content**: explicit `compact / flex / hero` contract. Replaces v2's universal `repeat(auto-fit, minmax(280px, 1fr))` which forced every tile to fill, creating dead space.

**ModCardV3 protected**: registered in §9 Frozen Components. The mod card itself is sacred; only the surrounding grid is free.

**UI Pro Max folded inline**: quantitative engineering rules (contrast, motion timing, virtualization, tabular figures, container queries) live in §11. One source of truth.

**Geist Pixel ornament system**: novel use — never as text, always as iconographic accent (polarity markers, dividers, status pips, section bullets). Anti-AI-slop because no other tool does this.

### 2026-05-29 — Mutalist Glow theme lock (preserved from v2)

Pivot from "Dark teal/cyan" because every Warframe community app uses Lotus cyan + Orokin gold. Tennoplan was visually indistinguishable from the genre default. Mutalist Glow leans into Heart of Deimos / Cambion Drift aesthetic — lore-coded, distinctive, themeable for future variants.

### 2026-04-25 — Component library principle (preserved)

CSS variables + reusable components. Theming = re-mapping color tokens, not redesigning components. Build once, theme everywhere.
